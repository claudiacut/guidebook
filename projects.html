HOW TO READ IT

GUIDEBOOK CARDS

The Guidebook contains a total of 15 guidelines,
generated from the definition of 6 macro-challenges and divided
according to the 4 user journey phases in which they are applied.

See the Guidelines

BEFORE THE USER TAKES ACTION

These guidelines must be considered for organizing interaction strategies enhancing orientation, thus helping the users before they take actions. This category contains 2 challenges and 5 guidelines.

Challenge 3

NAVIGATING THROUGH INTELLIGIBLE AND QUICK MECHANISMS

These giudelines must be applied for those interactions occurring while the user is navigating through the website, because they provide guidance on how to manage the navigation structure within the selected website.

Challenge 4

SUMMARIZING AND SEGMENTING THE PAGE CONTENT

This challenge contains 3 guidelines and provide guidance on how to manage content within the selected website, to respond to content access needs occurring during navigation.

WHEN SOMETHING IS WRONG

The guidelines included in this user journey phase should be applied when something is wrong in the navigation. They allow the user to correct or prevent actions and requests. This category contains 1 challenge and 4 guidelines.

Challenge 5

PROVIDING ACCESS TO CONVERSATION SCAFFOLDING INTENTS

The scaffolding commands encapsulate the functionality that the conversational agent must have by default, as they are instructions to generate commands that allow for the smoothest and most dynamic navigation experience possible.

TO ACCOMODATE USER'S EXPERIENCE
OVER TIME

The guidelines included in this design phase need to be applied and calibrated over time, as they relate to design features that depend on the user’s level of familiarity with navigation, their preferences, and their level of experience.
This category contains 1 challenge and 2 guidelines.

Challenge 6

CONTROLLING AND PERSONALIZING THE CONVERSATIONAL WEB

These guidelines can be defined at the time of building the CA, based on standard reports referring to the context of use of the CA and the identified target audience.
